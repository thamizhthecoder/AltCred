How It Works

ALT-CRED is a simple platform which helps to increase your possibilities to get a loan in 3 simple steps

Step 1: Join AltCred

Effortless Onboarding:Sign up using your smartphone without extensive paperwork.

Establish Your Digital Identity: Create your profile and invite trusted community members to endorse your credibility.

Step 2: Get Approved Using Your Transaction History & Community Data

Leverage Your Bank-Linked UPI Transactions: With your consent, AltCred securely accesses your UPI transaction history—which requires a linked bank account—along with your mobile recharge and bill payment records to assess your financial behavior.

Holistic Credit Assessment: These real-world financial insights are combined with community endorsements to create a comprehensive AltCred Score that truly reflects your trustworthiness.

Step 3: Get Approved and Access Nearby Banks

Seamless Loan Approval: Use your AltCred Score to apply for microloans directly through the app.

Broaden Your Financial Options: Once approved, approach partnered local banks and financial institutions, showcasing your enhanced profile and trust score to secure competitive loan terms.

Frequently Ask Questions

1. How do I join AltCred?

Joining AltCred is straightforward:

  • Sign Up via Mobile: Download our user-friendly web app to get started.
  • Create Your Profile Using Your Bank Account: Your bank account is essential because it’s linked to your UPI transactions, allowing you to build a verifiable financial identity
    • .
  • Get Endorsed: Invite trusted community members to endorse your credibility and help establish your AltCred Score
    • .

    2. Why is a bank account necessary for AltCred?

    A bank account is required because it enables UPI transactions, which serve as a key source of real-time financial data. This linkage allows AltCred to accurately capture your everyday financial behavior and build a reliable credit profile.

    3. What kind of financial data does AltCred use?

    AltCred uses non-traditional financial data such as:

  • UPI transaction history (bank-linked)
  • Mobile recharge records
  • Utility bill payment data
    • These data points help us assess your daily financial habits and determine your trustworthiness.

    4. How do community endorsements work?

    Community endorsements allow individuals you know—like friends, neighbors, or local leaders—to vouch for your financial responsibility. These endorsements complement your transaction data, providing a more complete picture of your creditworthiness.

    5. How is the AltCred Score calculated?

    Your AltCred Score is generated by combining:

  • Your bank-linked UPI transaction history
  • Other non-traditional financial data (mobile recharges, utility bills)
  • Peer and community endorsements
    • Advanced AI/ML models process this comprehensive data to produce a dynamic credit score that reflects your overall financial reliability.

    6. How secure is my data on AltCred?

    Security is a top priority at AltCred. We use secure technologies such as Firebase Authentication and robust databases like MongoDB to protect your data. All information is encrypted and managed in compliance with strict data protection standards.

    7. Who approves the loans once I have an AltCred Score?

    While AltCred builds your credit profile through transaction data and endorsements, the final loan approval is handled by our partnered financial institutions and local banks. Your AltCred Score serves as a trusted indicator of your creditworthiness during their evaluation process.

    8. Is joining AltCred free?

    Yes, signing up and creating your profile on AltCred is completely free. Our mission is to provide an accessible pathway for financial inclusion without any initial cost for users.

    9. Where can I get additional support or information about AltCred?

    For further assistance, you can reach out to our support team through the in-app help section or visit our website’s support page. Our dedicated team is here to help with any questions or concerns you may have about using AltCred.
